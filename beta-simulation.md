# Swanblade: Competitive Intelligence + Beta Simulation

## Direct Competitors

| Competitor | Price | What They Do | Swanblade's Edge |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Suno/Udio** | $8-30/mo | Generate music from text | Can't start from YOUR audio. Prompt-and-pray. |
| **LANDR** | $13-25/mo | AI mastering + distribution | Black box, no transformation, low ceiling |
| **iZotope Ozone** | $20-500 | Desktop mastering suite | Local only, no cloud GPU, no generative AI |
| **Splice** | $20/mo | Sample library + plugins | Samples, not transformation |
| **AudioShake** | $20-99/mo | Stem separation only | One trick. No re-synthesis. |
| **Stable Audio** | Free/enterprise | Text-to-audio API | Stability AI chaos, no transformation pipeline |
| **AIVA** | $15-49/mo | MIDI/score generation | Classical-leaning, no audio-in workflow |
| **Boomy** | $10-30/mo | One-click song maker | Toy-grade quality |
| **Kits.AI** | $12-60/mo | Voice cloning + stems | Voice-centric, no GPU processing chain |

**The gap**: Every competitor is either a **generator** (create from nothing) or a **polisher** (master finished audio). Nobody occupies the **transformation** middle -- take your audio, sculpt it through multiple AI engines with fine control. That's Swanblade alone.

## What Makes It 12/10

**10/10 features** (ship these):
- Chain Recipes: save multi-step processing pipelines, one-click re-apply to new tracks
- Dolby Atmos / spatial audio AI upmix (LANDR charges $100/track for this)
- Provenance certificates for sync licensing (proves human creative direction)

**12/10 features** (category-defining):
- **Sound DNA Fingerprint**: Upload your catalog (50-500 tracks), Swanblade builds a statistical model of YOUR signature sound. Every new track gets "match to my DNA" as an option. This is not reference mastering -- it's a model of your entire body of work. Creates permanent lock-in.
- **Encrypted Stem Collaboration**: Share stems that are only playable inside Swanblade. Collaborators hear but can't download raw files. Watermarked with C2PA provenance.
- **Taste Learning from Feedback**: The thumbs up/down system builds a per-user preference model. Over time: "show me what I'll like" becomes possible. Compounding data moat.

## Unexpected Markets (Good Fit)

**1. Forensic Audio / Law Enforcement** -- Score: 7/10
> Need to enhance degraded recordings, separate voices from noise, super-resolve low-quality audio. AudioSR + Demucs + spectral matching is exactly this pipeline. "For 10: court-admissible processing chain documentation. For 12: speaker isolation from noisy surveillance audio."

**2. Audiobook / Podcast Networks (Spotify, Audible)** -- Score: 7/10
> Need to match audio quality across 100s of episodes recorded in different environments. Reference mastering against a "house sound" template. "For 10: batch processing 500 episodes. For 12: automatic room tone matching so every episode sounds like the same studio."

**3. Museum / Installation Artists** -- Score: 8/10
> Need to create hours of evolving ambient soundscapes from source material. VampNet at low temperature with long duration = infinite variation from a seed recording. "For 10: continuous generation mode (hours of output). For 12: generative installation mode -- the AI never repeats, creating unique audio for each visitor."

**4. Sample Pack Creators** -- Score: 9/10
> Need to create 200+ unique variations from 20 source recordings. VampNet + different seeds + different stems = instant sample pack. "For 10: metadata tagging (BPM, key, mood) on every output. For 12: one-click Splice/LANDR distribution integration."

**5. Hearing Aid / Accessibility Companies** -- Score: 6/10
> AudioSR and spectral matching could enhance degraded audio for hearing-impaired users in real-time. "For 10: real-time processing under 50ms. For 12: personalized frequency boosting based on audiogram data."
